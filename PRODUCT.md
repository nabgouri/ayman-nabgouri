# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers at product companies, and founders at early-stage startups. They arrive through a link in a job application, a LinkedIn profile, or a referral. They are scanning quickly, deciding within seconds whether Ayman is worth a conversation. They are looking for signals of taste, initiative, and real shipping ability, not a list of skills.

## Product Purpose

Convert first-time viewers into reaching out. The portfolio is the first work sample, not a resume supplement. Its existence and quality are a direct signal of what Ayman builds when given creative control. Success is a recruiter or founder who finishes scrolling and thinks "this person builds things I want on my team."

## Brand Personality

Bold, expressive, ambitious. A developer who has taste and knows it, who builds with intention, and who is looking for the next hard problem, not just the next job.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev portfolio templates: gradient blobs, animated skills progress bars, identical icon-heading-text card grids, floating tech badges. These signal someone who followed a tutorial.
- Over-animated flash: particle systems, cursor trails, neon glow effects. Loud without meaning.
- Resume-as-website: a LinkedIn timeline with a contact form. No personality, no stakes.
- Artsy / experimental to the point of confusion: impresses other designers, alienates engineering hiring managers.

## Design Principles

1. **The portfolio is the pitch deck.** Every design decision is a work sample. Sloppy spacing or stock patterns undercut the resume.
2. **Bold choices, not decoration.** Expressive means committed — a clear aesthetic point of view, not ornament piled on a neutral base.
3. **Ambitious posture.** The site should feel like it was made by someone choosing their next role, not applying to everything. Signal selectivity.
4. **Show before telling.** Project depth and visual quality make the case before copy does. Let the work introduce the person.
5. **Synthesize references, never copy them.** Draw from Vercel, Awwwards craft, and Stripe Press editorial energy, but produce something that could only be Ayman's.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a baseline: sufficient contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, semantic HTML, screen reader support. Reduced motion (`prefers-reduced-motion`) should be respected for all animations given the animation-forward design direction.
